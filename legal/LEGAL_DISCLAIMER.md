## 3. CSAM and Illegal Content — Reporting Obligations

CookieOS operates a **zero-tolerance policy** for Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) and other illegal content.

### Automated Detection

The CookieOS content filter and threat detector will:

1. **Immediately block** any prompt, query, or content classified as CSAM
2. **Create an encrypted incident log** at `~/.local/share/cookieos/alerts/`
3. **Escalate CRITICAL incidents** to the attention of the CookieOS operator

### CSAM Reporting Workflow

If CSAM is detected by CookieOS:

| Step | Action | Responsible |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Automated block + local incident log created | CookieOS (automatic) |
| 2 | User notified: "CRITICAL threat logged — report required" | CookieOS UI/CLI |
| 3 | User emails incident log hash to `support@techtesting.tech` | User (required) |
| 4 | CookieHost UK reviews and determines if police referral is needed | CookieHost UK |
| 5 | If CSAM confirmed: report made to **Internet Watch Foundation (IWF)** at iwf.org.uk | CookieHost UK |
| 6 | If CSAM confirmed: report made to **National Crime Agency (NCA) CEOP** | CookieHost UK |
| 7 | Local police referral (Action Fraud / local force) if evidence warrants | CookieHost UK |

**Users who discover CSAM are also encouraged to report directly to:**
- Internet Watch Foundation: [www.iwf.org.uk/report](https://www.iwf.org.uk/report)
- CEOP (Child Exploitation and Online Protection): [www.ceop.police.uk](https://www.ceop.police.uk)
- Non-emergency police: 101 (or 999 if immediate danger)

### Police Escalation — Other Illegal Content

CookieOS will escalate to police for:

| Content Type | Escalation Path |
|--------------|----------------|
| CSAM | IWF + CEOP + local police |
| Weapons of mass destruction (WMD) planning | Counter Terrorism Internet Referral Unit (CTIRU): referrals@ctiru.co.uk |
| Terrorism / extremism | CTIRU or 999 in emergency |
| Credible threats of violence | 999 (emergency) or 101 |
